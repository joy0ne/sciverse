Privacy Policy — Sciverse

Last updated: 26 May 2026

Sciverse is a free, offline-first science education app. This policy describes, plainly and honestly, what data the app handles.

Short version

Sciverse does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. The app works entirely on your device with no external network requests.

What data is stored on your device

Sciverse stores a small amount of information locally in your browser/app storage to remember your preferences:

  • Theme (dark/light), interface language, and which topics you've opened (a tick mark on visited cards).
  • An offline conversation history with the built-in study assistant.

This data never leaves your device. You can delete it at any time by clearing your browser data or uninstalling the app.

What data is NOT collected

  • No name, email, phone number, or account.
  • No location data.
  • No device identifiers (no advertising ID, no IMEI).
  • No analytics, telemetry, or crash reports.
  • No cookies sent to any server.

Network connections

Sciverse makes no network requests. The single HTML file contains all topics, articles, simulations and the offline assistant. The app's Content Security Policy explicitly blocks outbound connections (connect-src 'none'). You can verify this with any browser network inspector.

Third parties

No third-party services, SDKs, ad networks, or trackers are integrated into the app.

Children

The app does not collect any information, so it does not collect information from children. The content is suitable for ages 13+.

Changes to this policy

If the policy ever changes, the updated version will appear at the same URL with a new date. Material changes will also be noted in the app's release notes.

Contact

Questions or concerns? Open an issue at the project's GitHub repository.
